César E. Chávez

Important Information and Achievements

  • César Chávez was born in Yuma, Arizona on March 31, 1927.
  • He was born to a Mexican-America family.
  • Was a manual laborer prior to spending two years in the U.S. Navy.
  • He got involved in the Community Service Organization in California, where he became their national director in 1959.
  • He left the CSO to co-found the National Farm Workers Association in 1962.
  • During this time, he helped launch an insurance scheme, a credit union, and the El Macriado newspaper all for farmworkers.
  • He organized strikes among farmworkers, with the Delano grape strike of 1965 - 1970 being particularly successful.
  • Merged NFWA with Larry Itliong's Agricultural Workers Organizing Committee to form the United Farm Workers labor union.
  • Used Ramon Catholic symbolism in his campaigns.
  • Alliance with Jerry Brown, California's then governor, allowed him to help get the California Agricultural Labor Relations Act of 1975 passed.
  • Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom posthumously in 1994.
